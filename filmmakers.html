  • TIM PURDIE: CO-PRODUCER & CO-DIRECTOR

    Tim is the Owner/Director of Teepee Media, a small independent production house primarily producing videos for the educational, social justice and environmental sectors. He is a graduate of the Advanced Diploma of Film & TV at Footscray City Film School (2005). Tim has previously worked as a First Assistant Director and Production Manager on several independent feature films and large corporate video productions. As well as writing and directing his own award winning short films, Tim enjoys working with refugee groups across Melbourne assisting them to tell their stories through film and theatre. He co-directed the critically acclaimed show 'Returning Sudanese' at the Melbourne International Comedy Festival in 2009 where issues concerning racism, migration and cultural differences were explored through humour by a local comedian and Sudanese refugees. Since 2010, Tim has been producing short documentary style videos for organisations such as The Victorian Equal Opportunity and Human Rights Commission, Monash University, Trust for Nature and Rotary. Tim is also skilled and experienced in website design and post-production editing.

  • ANDREW BLOGG: CO-PRODUCER & CO-DIRECTOR

    Andrew has been involved with film and video production since graduating with an Advanced Diploma of Film & TV from Footscray City Film School (2004). His graduating film 'Chay', the story of Cambodian brothers coping with grief, was shown at the prestigious New York Short Film Festival. In 2006 Andrew produced a 3 hour, 6 part, educational documentary series called "The Secret Life of Genes" for James Cook University in Townsville QLD. "The Secret Life of Genes" was designed to educate first year medical students on the basics of genetics. Andrew used socks, card games and various other methods to demonstrate the complexities of genetics in a simple and easy to understand way that can be also watched, understood and enjoyed by all. Andrew flew a team of 5 including a presenter to the location in QLD for filming. "The Secret Life of Genes" was nominated for an ATOM Award (Australian Teachers Of Media Awards) in 2007. Andrew currently produces, writes and directs documentary style, educational programs and is also a tutor at the Victorian College of the Arts, School of Film and TV.

  • GAYE MILLER: CO-PRODUCER, RESEARCHER

    Gaye has a strong spiritual connection with Cambodia and spends as much time as possible there. Whilst in Cambodia, she works closely with several orphanages as a sponsor and English teacher. She pays university fees and is a mentor and 'Mother' to a group of desperately poor students. Her greatest challenge has been getting shipping containers into Cambodia. Through sheer perseverance and a never say die attitude, she succeeded where big corporations had previously failed. She then faced the logistical night mare of organising a fleet of trucks and drivers to deliver the contents to far flung rural areas. Gaye has single handedly set up a library, a medical clinic, taught English, supplied bicycles so students can attend school, delivered thousands of mosquito nets in malaria prone areas, paid university fees and living allowances, supplied surgical equipment and much more. In 2008 Gaye was nominated for Australian of the Year for her humanitarian work in Cambodia.

  • HOM CHHORN: CO-PRODUCER, NARRATOR & INTERVIEWER

    Hom migrated to Australia with most of his remaining family members after surviving the atrocities of the Khmer Rouge and Camp 32. After college, Hom went on to work for SBS Television, most notably on assignment in Cambodia with Dateline. He remained in Cambodia for 8 years, where he Advised UNDP/TVK crews in the production of TV newsmagazines, focusing on women and micro credit schemes, birth spacing, rural development, De-mining, HIV/AIDS, and environmental issues. Other duties included training TVK staff on television program development, and how to seek funding. Hom co-produced a short Documentary 'Have Gun, Have Problem' which was funded by The European Union as part of their Weapon Reduction Programme. Over 250,000 Cambodian villagers viewed this important documentary on the dangers of weapons. As well as producing educational videos on Water and Sanitation, Hom also produced three Cambodian Television series, two of which are still running 10 years later. One of these shows 'Cambodian Voices' was funded by NDI under the Madeline Albright funds USAID and AUSAID. Hom is currently working for the BBC in Cambodia.
